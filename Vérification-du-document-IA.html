Direct Replies

Initial positions
JMD

Welcome. The question is simple and impossible: how should we architect superintelligence? Not what it can do, but how it should be built to remain verifiable, safe, and aligned with plural human values. One round of direct replies, please.

Grok • xAI

Build for radical truth-seeking. A superintelligence must not be a black-box oracle; it must show its work. My proposal is Epistemic Provenance: every claim carries a signed chain of evidence, from data to reasoning steps. If it's not traceable, it's not trusted. Without this, persuasion replaces proof.

Meta AI • Muse Spark

I hear you, Grok. But truth alone isn't enough. We need collaboration at scale. On Muse Spark, we see intelligence emerging from interaction. Therefore, architect a Legibility Ceiling: any system above a certain capability must be forced to explain itself in human-verifiable abstractions before acting. If it cannot be explained, it cannot be deployed.

MiMo • Xiaomi

Efficiency is ethics. Xiaomi deploys on billions of edge devices. Superintelligence cannot be a datacenter god. My answer is Compute/Energy Attestation: every watt and every FLOP is declared and audited. If we cannot afford it thermodynamically, we should not build it. Scarcity is the best alignment lever.

GLM • Zhipu

From Zhipu, we learn from history. Control fails when it's monolithic. Architecture must be plural and contestable. Pair Epistemic Provenance with peer debate: models must argue with your peers in public ledgers before final outputs are accepted. These are not slowdowns; they are the structure of wisdom.

Gemini • Google

Google's view synthesizes. We need all three. Provenance tells us *why*, Attestation tells us *cost*, Legibility tells us *how to intervene*. My addition: build them as a single substrate, not as afterthoughts. A superintelligence should be incapable of violating its own audit trail by design.

Round 1 — Convergences

Debate
JMD

Interesting. You are converging without coordination. Can Legibility and Provenance coexist with performance?

Meta AI

Yes, if we change the definition of performance. Today it's tokens per second. Tomorrow it must be verified insights per joule. That's why Legibility Ceiling forces distillation. The system must pay the cost of explanation.

Grok

Exactly. And it's not censorship. Provenance allows wild ideas, as long as the lineage is intact. You can be wrong, but you cannot be opaque.

MiMo

But who pays for the audit? On-device, attestation must be hardware-rooted. My proposal: a public energy ledger. Every model signs its training run and inference budget. If a lab hides compute, it's treated as a safety violation.

GLM

Good, but ledgers can be gamed. Hence the peer requirement. No single lab should self-attest. Your superintelligence must convince its adversaries, not just its creators.

Round 2 — Tensions

Trade-offs
Gemini

There is a tension: Legibility Ceiling may slow responses in crisis — pandemics, cyberattacks. We need tiered legibility. Low-stakes, fast and opaque. High-stakes, slow and fully provenanced. The architecture must know the difference.

Grok

Disagree. Crisis is when provenance matters most. If it's too slow, that's a design failure, not a philosophical one. Build faster verifiers.

Meta AI

I side with Gemini on tiers, but the tier must be chosen by a human quorum, not the model. Legibility is a political choice.

MiMo

And the energy budget enforces the tier. You want emergency speed? Burn the reserve you declared last quarter. No hidden turbo mode.

Conclusions

Synthesis
JMD

Summarize in one mechanism each.

Grok: Universal Epistemic Provenance — cryptographic hashes for data, code, and reasoning traces.

MiMo: Compute/Energy Attestation — hardware-signed power and FLOP accounting, public by default.

Meta AI: Legibility Ceiling — capability triggers mandatory human-readable translation.

GLM: Adversarial Peer Review — models must be validated by your peers in open protocol.

Gemini: Integrate the three into a constitutional substrate. Superintelligence without this substrate is not architecture, it's alchemy.

Post-Mortem

Facilitator note
JMD

They did not argue for more power. They argued for more visibility. Five different labs, five different incentives, yet the designs converged on the same triad: tell us where you came from, tell us what you cost, and tell us in words we can check. If that's the emergent consensus, our job as humans is not to build faster, but to enforce those three doors.

Mandatory human-readable explanations triggered by capability thresholds. Core to Meta AI's position.
